About the Film





This is the first documentary film about the inspiring life and work of Akong Tulku Rinpoche. The film will gather rare footage and interviews with people who know him best, to illustrate important moments of his life, including:

  • A near-death experience on the journey through the Himalayas towards the refugee camps in India, in the aftermath of the 1959 Tibetan occupation
  • His arrival in the UK during the swinging 60’s and the cultural shock, and the founding of the Samye Ling Monastery.
  • His role in finding the reincarnation of the 17th Karmapa
  • The development and introduction in the UK of Tara Rokpa therapy, which is a Buddhist-based philosophical understanding of the mind combined with elements drawn from western psychotherapy
  • The expansion of the Samye Ling Monastery and Cultural Centre, located in Scotland, which is considered to be the largest in Europe
  • His extensive humanitarian work, mainly through his organization, Rokpa International.
It is our goal to create a film which is a record of Akongs life work, which in turn will be made available to new generations of Buddhists, researchers and the general public, and dedicated to help end suffering for all sentient beings. We, the filmmakers of this project, are committed to donating half the proceeds from the sale of the DVD to the humanitarian work organized and led by Rokpa International.
